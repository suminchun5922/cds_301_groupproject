Abstract

This project investigates the ongoing shift in fashion retail by analyzing both macro-level market trends and micro-level consumer behaviors from two datasets. U.S. Census data shows that while traditional clothing retail only doubled from 1999 to 2023, the e-commerce sector expanded by over 2,500%. A consumer-level analysis of 3,900 online purchase records identifies the key driver of this surge: female shoppers in their 20s‚Äì30s, with a strong preference for ‚ÄòClothing‚Äô. Spending was found to be age-independent, while December consistently showed seasonal spikes, highlighting the importance of timing in online fashion sales.

Introduction

The fashion industry is undergoing a dramatic shift, widely termed the ‚ÄúE-commerce Revolution.‚Äù While the traditional U.S. clothing retail market has grown steadily since 1992, online retail has surged, redefining the structure of fashion commerce. This project aims to quantify this transformation by analyzing long-term market data and consumer behavior. We address two core questions:

  • Macro: How large is the e-commerce sector‚Äôs growth compared to traditional retail?
  • Micro: Who is driving this digital shift, and what defines their purchasing patterns?
